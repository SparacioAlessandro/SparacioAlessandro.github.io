About Me

I am a Research Scientist at the A*STAR Singapore Institute for Clinical Sciences (SICS), where my work is dedicated to advancing the field of mental health through research on cognition, mood, and sleep. My primary focus is on developing and validating cost-effective, non-pharmacological interventions that can be scaled to improve well-being for a broad population.

This commitment is exemplified by my work leading a pre-registered, multi-site study on self-administered mindfulness, which was published in Nature Human Behaviour. Involving 37 international sites and over 2,200 participants, this project established new benchmarks for rigor in the field. My research journey began during my joint Ph.D. at the University of Grenoble Alpes and Swansea University, where I investigated stress regulation processes through large-scale meta-analyses and collaborative projects.

I am a strong advocate for Open Science and am dedicated to conducting research with the highest level of scientific integrity to contribute meaningfully to global mental health and health equity.

Current Projects

The REFORM Trial

As Principal Investigator, I am leading a new multi-site study on self-administered mindfulnes. This project aims to build resilience across diverse socioeconomic contexts by investigating the optimal ratio of formal vs. informal self-administered mindfulness practice. Using an adaptive Bayesian design, we seek to provide definitive, actionable guidance for the future of digital mental health.
